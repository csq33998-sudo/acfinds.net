What an AllChinaBuy spreadsheet is used for

An AllChinaBuy spreadsheet is usually a curated shopping reference: product links, streetwear categories, seller notes, QC reminders, sizing comments, shipping cost planning, and item ideas collected before a buyer commits to an agent order. It is useful for discovery, but it should not replace checking the live listing, warehouse photos, measurements, route limits, and final shipping cost.

Common mistakes

  • Treating a spreadsheet row as final proof. Open the live listing and check whether the item still exists.
  • Assuming old links are current. Batches, prices, colors, and seller inventory can change quickly.
  • Skipping measurement photos because the listing image looks good. Fit issues are easier to catch in the warehouse.
  • Building a haul only from hype pieces instead of items that work together in real outfits.

FAQ

What are AllChinaBuy spreadsheet finds?

Usually curated product references or rows people share. They are useful for discovery, but you should still open the live listing and verify QC before paying through your agent.

What do people mean by AllChinaBuy spreadsheet links?

How do I check AllChinaBuy QC photos?

Compare the warehouse photos with the seller listing, then inspect stitching, shape, stains, detail placement, color accuracy, and measurements before shipping. See the AllChinaBuy QC photos guide.

How do I estimate AllChinaBuy shipping cost?

Check item weight, package volume, shoe boxes, jacket bulk, route limits, and whether each item is worth sending. See the shipping cost guide.

Are AllChinaBuy size charts reliable?

Use size charts as measurements, not labels. Compare shoulder, chest, sleeve, waist, inseam, and insole length with items you own. See the size chart guide.
