How to use an AllChinaBuy spreadsheet

A practical workflow for turning spreadsheet links and product finds into safer agent purchases: search, verify, measure, inspect, estimate shipping cost, and ship only the items that still make sense.

1. Open the spreadsheet link and identify the real listing

Do not judge a find by the spreadsheet row alone. Open the product page, confirm the item is still available, check current colors and sizes, and make sure the listing photos match the item you intended to buy. This matters for AllChinaBuy product links, fashion finds, sneakers, hoodies, jackets, pants, bags, jerseys, and accessories.

2. Use AllChinaBuy QC photos before international shipping

  • Compare shape, stitching, print placement, label details, color, and material texture against the listing photos.
  • Ask for measurement photos when fit is the main risk, especially for shoes, hoodies, pants, and jackets.
  • Exchange or return before international shipping if the item is clearly wrong, damaged, or a different variant.

3. Read AllChinaBuy size charts like measurements, not labels

Do not rely only on S, M, L labels. Compare bust, shoulder, sleeve, waist, length, inseam, leg opening, and shoe insole measurements with items you already own. If a spreadsheet note says "size up", still confirm the actual chart and warehouse measurement photos.

4. Plan AllChinaBuy shipping cost before paying

Group items by urgency, season, and weight. Shoes, heavy jackets, and boxed accessories can change shipping economics quickly. A focused haul with confirmed QC is usually better than a large cart built from random spreadsheet links.

5. Browse live finds after you know what to check

Once you understand the checks above, browse live AllChinaBuy spreadsheet-style finds by product category and keep notes on size, seller, QC risk, shipping estimate, return risk, and item priority.
